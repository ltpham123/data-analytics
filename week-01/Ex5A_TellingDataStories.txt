I'm writing about Lakes and Oceans as I love exploring scaling as a concept. The information presented in Lakes and Oceans describes and compares elevation and topography between various bodies of water. The xkcd webcomic by Randall Munroe illustrations some facts alongside just sea depth, like the location of the titanic, or how far humankind have dug down from surface level. One fact I find facinating is that although it seems like we've explored lots of the ocean and/or Earth's composition, we barely have touched what we know about our own planet. The Kola Bore, as noted in the infographic, is about 12.0km, which is only 0.2% of the way to Earth's core. It is insane when we compare our accomplishments to our understanding of celestial bodies, as discussed in class, when talking about a very, very small portion/sample of the "whole."
